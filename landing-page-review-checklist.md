# Landing Page Conversion Review Checklist

Use this worksheet before publishing a new landing page, refreshing an old campaign, or handing a page to a designer, copywriter, or client for review.

The awesome list remains public and CC0. If this checklist saves you time, you can support the project by buying the optional $12 supporter copy: https://buy.stripe.com/00w14p6Jz7d55qe5ZA8so03

## 1. Offer Clarity

- [ ] The headline names the product, service, or outcome without requiring extra context.
- [ ] The subheadline explains who it is for and what changes after using it.
- [ ] The first screen includes the primary call to action.
- [ ] The page avoids generic claims such as "all-in-one", "revolutionary", or "next generation" unless they are immediately proven.
- [ ] The offer makes clear whether the visitor is buying, booking, joining a waitlist, downloading, or contacting sales.

## 2. Audience Match

- [ ] The page names the target customer in the language they would use.
- [ ] The pain points match a specific situation, not a broad demographic.
- [ ] The examples, screenshots, or proof points resemble the visitor's real workflow.
- [ ] The page handles the most common objection before the main call to action repeats.
- [ ] The page avoids features that are irrelevant to the target visitor's first decision.

## 3. Proof

- [ ] The page has at least one concrete proof element: testimonial, metric, logo, case study, sample output, product screenshot, or demo.
- [ ] Testimonials mention a before-and-after result, not just generic praise.
- [ ] Metrics include a source, time period, or enough context to be credible.
- [ ] If the product is new, the page uses founder credibility, prototype evidence, or transparent early-access framing instead of fake certainty.
- [ ] Any trust badges, compliance claims, or security statements are accurate.

## 4. Call To Action

- [ ] The primary CTA uses a verb that matches the next step.
- [ ] Secondary CTAs do not compete visually with the primary CTA.
- [ ] The CTA appears after the hero, after proof, and near the final section.
- [ ] Forms ask only for fields needed at this stage.
- [ ] Button labels avoid vague text such as "Submit" or "Learn More" when a clearer action exists.

## 5. Message Flow

- [ ] The page answers: what is it, who is it for, why now, why trust it, and what happens next.
- [ ] Sections are ordered by visitor decision-making, not internal company structure.
- [ ] Each section has one job and one clear takeaway.
- [ ] Repeated claims become more specific each time they appear.
- [ ] The page removes details that create more questions than confidence.

## 6. Visual Review

- [ ] The first screen shows the actual product, result, interface, venue, or deliverable when possible.
- [ ] Text does not overlap images, buttons, nav, or cards on mobile.
- [ ] Mobile pages have no horizontal overflow.
- [ ] Screenshots are readable at the displayed size.
- [ ] Color contrast supports reading in bright and dim environments.

## 7. Performance And Tracking

- [ ] The page loads quickly on a mobile connection.
- [ ] Analytics capture page views, CTA clicks, form starts, form completions, and checkout starts where relevant.
- [ ] Thank-you or success pages are tracked separately.
- [ ] UTM parameters survive redirects and checkout links.
- [ ] Error states and abandoned forms are observable.

## 8. Final Pass

- [ ] Read the full page aloud and remove words you would not say to a customer.
- [ ] Click every CTA on desktop and mobile.
- [ ] Test the page on the smallest supported mobile viewport.
- [ ] Confirm legal, pricing, refund, privacy, and contact details are accurate.
- [ ] Save the final page URL, screenshot, analytics event names, and owner in the campaign record.

## Quick Scoring

Score each area from 0 to 2.

| Area | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Offer clarity | unclear | understandable | specific and compelling |
| Audience match | generic | partly specific | tightly matched |
| Proof | absent | present but weak | concrete and credible |
| CTA | vague | clear but uneven | clear and repeated |
| Message flow | scattered | mostly ordered | persuasive sequence |
| Visual review | distracting | acceptable | polished and responsive |
| Tracking | missing | partial | decision-ready |

Recommended threshold before launch: 10 or higher out of 14, with no zero in offer clarity, proof, or CTA.
